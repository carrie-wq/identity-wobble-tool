The Identity Wobble™
Mapping Tool

A 5-minute orientation process for navigating growth, visibility, and identity shifts — without losing yourself in them.

Step 1 of 6
Before We Begin

This tool is not here to fix anything.

It's here to orient you.

When you're in an identity shift — letting go of who you were, growing into who you're becoming — your mind does something predictable: it turns the discomfort into a verdict about you.

Mapping the wobble interrupts that. It turns "something is wrong with me" into "ah — this is the phase I'm in."

Answer honestly. There are no wrong responses. This is for you.

Section 01 — Context

What's actually happening right now?

Select everything that's true. (More than one is common — that's the point.)

Section 02 — Your Wobble Voice

What does your wobble sound like?

Select the thoughts that feel most familiar — the ones that have been loudest lately.

Section 03 — Your Escape Routes

When the wobble peaks, what do you reach for?

These are not bad habits. They're familiar safety behaviors. Naming them means you don't act on them unconsciously.

Section 04 — Grounding

Write one thing you still know to be true about your work — even if you can't feel it right now.

Don't try to make it impressive. Just notice what's still there beneath the noise.

Section 05 — Locate Yourself

Which phase feels most like where you are right now?

Every wobble moves through five phases. You might feel elements of more than one — pick the one that feels closest.

Phase 01
Letting Go — I'm releasing an old identity, role, or way of proving my value. It hasn't fully landed yet. I feel the loss more than the gain.
Phase 02
Exposure — I'm being seen differently. Less execution, more presence. The imposter thoughts are loudest here.
Phase 03
Meaning Collapse — Nothing feels like it's working because I don't know what "working" looks like in this new version of me.
Phase 04
Nervous System Alarm — The pull to retreat feels most rational right now. I'm tempted to go back to what's familiar.
Phase 05
Integration — Something has quietly shifted. I feel steadier, less dependent on external proof. The work is starting to feel like mine.

Your Containment Rules

While you're here, you do not:

  • Decide whether your work "works" or has value
  • Assume impact equals immediate applause or measurable proof
  • Abandon a direction because the nervous system is uncomfortable
  • Rewrite your story from inside the fear
  • Seek certainty from people who didn't witness the full picture
  • Make permanent decisions from temporary feelings
